10 Iconic Goa's Eats & Drinks That Every Visitor Must Try

From Goa's famous Seafood to the exotic Mediterranean food, Goa by the Bay offers a bevy of famous eats that you can't experience anywhere else.

1

Vinayak Family Restaurant

classic restaurant from Assagao, Vinayak Family Restaurant is quite popular with both tourists and locals, who swear by its legendary fish thali. Lunch time is frenzied, and expecting servers to take notice of you (and your never-ending orders of beer and Batter Fried Calamari or Bombil Rava Fry), is a game of perseverance, but everything that makes it to your table is worth the wait. It’s a great way to see an unusually hectic side of Goa with a well-stocked bar and a view of the nearby fields in tow.

Location: Sacol Waddo, Assagao, Goa
2

Roboto

In a quieter street in Calangute sits this very new, very excellent little restaurant, where local produce makes its way into Japanese cuisine – into miso soups and gyoza and more. That is, you'll get everything but sushi at unique izakaya. Order up a gin cocktail – we'd recommend the Seijaku (gin with homemade sencha tea cordial) or even the simple but genius mix that is Bees and Flowers – and a few plates of some the delectable beef tataki (a must-try) and chashu pork belly, and settle in to savour the kawaii art all around you.

Location: Near Dolphin Circle, Calangute, Goa
3

Martin's Corner

A crowd favourite, Martin's Corner is a giant of the Goan dining scene, both in space and time. It's been around since the 1980s and is huge enough to be the landmark that Betalbatim has come to be known by. Now you can't be a crowd favourite without presenting a menu of crowd pleasers: On offer are the greatest hits from every mainstream world cuisine, but try to skip the butter chicken and try some kingfish balchao - or shark ambotik, if you're feeling adventurous.

Location: Binwaddo, Salcette, Betalbatim, Goa
4

Thalassa

Hands down one of the most popular restaurants in Goa, this Greek restaurant grew from being a modest beachside cafe to a full-fledged taverna, before moving to Siolim some years ago. Who would have thought that they’d seamlessly make the move from their legendary Vagator spot without making a dent in their loyal fanbase, but here we are! Thalassa continues to remain synonymous with sundowners and a hit among tourists for providing the best photo-ops. If you’re there for an easy evening with friends, go straight for the wine and souvlakis along with ordering a side of their Lamb Meatballs in Red Sauce and Veg Moussaka.

Location: Vaddy Siolim, Goa
5

Ginger

Ginger is the best place for thali lovers. They provide best fish thalis in Madgaon with 1 large fish fry, 1 Roti, 1 veg curry, 2 non veg gravy dishes along with rice, solkadhi and salad. The quantity of thali is more than enough and food is very delicious. This is definitely a must visit place when you are in Madgao.

Location: Madgao Station, Goa
6

Fisherman’s Wharf, Cavelossim

As soon as you enter this place, the one obviously aesthetically pleasing bit is its sea-facing seating area. It’s an iconic marina style restaurant that gives a great view of the river Sal and the Cavelossim village from your table. The grilled fishes and lobster with Goan masalas accompanied with their signature margarita are to die for. Fantastic food along with the prompt and friendly service over here takes your fine-dine experience a notch higher. Though heavy on the pockets, the taste that it offers lingers longer in your mouth.

Location: Cavelossim, Goa
7

Souza Lobo, Goa

A dining institution standing right on the Calangute beach since 1932, Souza Lobo is one of the oldest restaurants that Goa has seen. True to its motto “traditional recipes live here”, the restaurant has been managed by generations together ensuring that the legacy is carried forward. They also have live music in the evenings to keep the diners entertained. Over the years, one thing that has still not changed is the authentic taste of the food served here.

Location: Calangute, Goa
8

Pousada By The Beach

Located on the quieter and lesser frequented beach strip between Calangute and Baga, Pousada By The Beach is a serene and peaceful place to sit and enjoy personalised service, impressive food and refreshing drinks. While the place has a simplistic vibe to it, their daytime menu is bona fide fine-dining. Their Prawn Cocktail is among the best you’ll taste in Goa, and we also recommend their Grilled Fish, tangy Pork Vindaloo and a spicy Pork Xacuti.

Location: Calangute, Goa
9

Zeebop

Arguably one of the most famous shacks in Goa, Zeebop draws people from all over Goa. If you want to do away with crowded spots, Zeebop offers the perfect setting with its quaint and basic sea-view seating and delicious seafood. Ask them for the fresh catch of the day and the staff comes carrying the seafood for you to pick. You can choose the preparation (in garlic/lemon butter or Recheado crumb fried) and relax. Their Tandoori sea-food selection is highly sought after, namely their Tandoori Pomfret and Fish Tikkas which you can pair with a pint of beer.

Location: Utorda,Salcete, Goa
10

O Coqueiro

A casual dining restaurant in Alto Porvorim (North Goa), the Portuguese Goan restaurant is a must-visit for anyone on a culinary journey in Goa. What started out as a tiny, rustic kitchen back in the ’60s with a fantastic Chicken Cafreal now offers various authentic, Goan fare. The airy restaurant features rustic architecture and interiors with a contemporary feel. The must-tries (apart from the Chicken Cafreal) include the Stuffed Crab, the Xacutis, their beef options, and alcohol-infused desserts! The best ways to experience O Coqueriro is either during live screenings of sporting events or during one of their regular food festivals known to throw light on different aspects of Goan cuisine.

Location: Alto Porvorim, Penha de França, Goa
